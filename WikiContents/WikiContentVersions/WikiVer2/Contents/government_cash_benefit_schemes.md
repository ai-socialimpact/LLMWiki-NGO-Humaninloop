## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer
<executive_brief_block>
### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: Government Cash Benefit Schemes | Milestone:: Beneficiary Eligibility & Application | Action:: Information Extraction] This document provides a detailed overview of two key Indian government schemes that offer direct cash benefits to pregnant and lactating mothers: the Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY) and the Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY). The PMMVY aims to provide partial wage compensation to women for rest before and after delivery. For the first living child, it provides ₹5,000 in two installments, contingent on early pregnancy registration, antenatal checkups, and child vaccination. For a second child, if it is a girl, a single installment of ₹6,000 is provided after birth registration and vaccination. The Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY) provides a cash incentive for delivering in a government hospital to eligible women from BPL families or Scheduled Castes/Tribes, for up to two live births. In Maharashtra, this amount is ₹700 for rural areas and ₹600 for urban areas, deposited into the mother's bank account within 7 days of delivery. Both schemes require specific documentation, including Aadhaar cards and bank account details, and involve registration at government health facilities like Anganwadi Centres.

### B. Explicit Architectural Scope Parameters
[Target:: Government Cash Benefit Schemes | Milestone:: Beneficiary Eligibility & Application | Action:: Information Extraction] This document is strictly limited to government schemes that provide direct monetary transfers (cash benefits) to pregnant women and lactating mothers. The focus is exclusively on the financial assistance component of these maternity benefit programs.
