### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] This document provides exhaustive dietary guidelines for pregnant women, based on recommendations from the Ministry of Family Health and Welfare and other health initiatives. A healthy pregnancy diet requires eating one extra meal per day, or an additional 350 calories from the second trimester, and consuming four to five small, nutritious meals daily. Key principles include eating a diverse, balanced diet rich in proteins, vitamins, minerals, and calories while avoiding junk food. Essential food groups include dairy (milk, curd), fresh fruits and vegetables, whole grains, pulses, and legumes. Specific micronutrients are critical: Iron for hemoglobin production, Folic Acid to prevent neural tube defects, Calcium for bone development, Iodine for brain development, Vitamin B12 for red blood cell formation, Vitamin D for calcium absorption, and Omega-3 fatty acids for fetal brain and vision development. Standard supplementation is mandatory, including Iron-Folic Acid (IFA) tablets (one daily) and Calcium Carbonate tablets (twice daily), which should be taken at separate times to ensure proper absorption. Hydration is crucial, with a recommendation of over 2 liters of fluids daily. Food safety is paramount, involving thorough hand washing, proper cleaning of produce, cooking food well, and avoiding specific items like alcohol, tobacco, raw eggs, unpasteurized milk, and limiting caffeine. The document also outlines recommended weight gain based on pre-pregnancy BMI (10-12 kg for normal BMI) and provides sample daily meal plans.

## 3. Exhaustive List of Facts
<facts_block>
### General Dietary Principles
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] During pregnancy, it is necessary to eat one extra meal in addition to your regular diet.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] Pregnant women should consume four to five small meals a day, including healthy snacks.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] Pregnant women should avoid junk food and foods with very high content of fat, sugar, or salt, like potato chips, achar, mithai, samosa, and kachori.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] Meals should be rich in proteins, vitamins, minerals, and calories.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] A balanced diet consisting of a variety of foods helps in the growth of the fetus and increases haemoglobin levels.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] A proper diet ensures the baby's growth and prepares the mother's body for delivery and breastfeeding.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] The "first 1,000 days" (conception to child's 2nd birthday) is a crucial period shaping the child's future.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] Poor nutritional status, including low-calorie/protein/micronutrient intake or high-fat, high-sugar, high-salt (HFSS) diets, can negatively affect fetal growth.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] The concept of a "Tiranga Thali" or "Rainbow Thali" (colourful plate) with green vegetables, grains, and fruits is promoted for a balanced diet.

### Recommended Weight Gain
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] Weight gain during pregnancy depends on the Body Mass Index (BMI) at conception.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] Women with a normal BMI (18.5–23 kg/m²) should aim to gain 10–12 kg.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] Underweight women (BMI <18.5 kg/m²) should increase their dietary intake and have their weight gain monitored closely.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] Overweight women (BMI 23–27.5 kg/m²) and obese women (BMI >27.5 kg/m²) should aim to gain not more than 5–9 kg.

### Calorie and Protein Requirements
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Pregnancy:** Pregnant women require an additional 350 calories daily from the second trimester onwards.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Pregnancy:** An additional 8g of protein is required during the second trimester and 18g during the third trimester.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Lactation (First 6 months):** An additional 600 calories and 13.6g of protein are required daily.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Lactation (Next 6 months):** An additional 520 calories and 10.6g of protein are required daily.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] Grains such as cereals and millets should contribute about 45% of the total daily energy requirement.

### Hydration and Fiber
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] It is advised to consume plenty of fluids, over 2 liters per day, including water and other beverages.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] A diet rich in fiber (around 25g/1000 Kcal) from whole grain cereals, pulses, and vegetables helps prevent constipation.

### Food Groups and Sources
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Proteins:** Found in lentils, milk, eggs, meat, fish, chicken, pulses, legumes (kidney beans, black beans, peas), and nuts. A handful (45 gm) of peanuts and 2 cups of lentils can fulfill daily protein requirements for vegetarians.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Carbohydrates:** Found in rice, wheat, and grains, providing necessary energy.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Fats:** Found in nuts, butter, and oils, providing long-term energy storage.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Dairy:** Milk, curd, buttermilk, and paneer are rich in calcium, proteins, and vitamins.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Fruits and Vegetables:** Fresh, seasonal fruits and vegetables provide essential vitamins and minerals. Green leafy vegetables are rich in iron and folic acid.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Whole Grains:** Bajra, jowar, and brown rice are recommended.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] Good quality protein can be derived from an appropriate combination of cereals and pulses in a 3:1 ratio.

### Micronutrient: Iron
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Importance:** Vital for producing haemoglobin, preventing anemia, supporting the child’s cognitive development, and carrying oxygen to the mother and fetus.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Animal Sources:** Liver (in moderation), lean meats (chicken, fish), eggs.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Plant Sources:** Green leafy vegetables (spinach, fenugreek/methi, red spinach, cabbage), legumes (lentils, chickpeas, beans), whole grains, dried fruits (raisins, dates, apricots), nuts and seeds (sesame, pumpkin), jaggery (Gur), peas, and white beans.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Absorption:** Vitamin C enhances the absorption of non-heme iron (from plants). Consume Vitamin C-rich foods like citrus fruits (oranges, lemon), guava, tomatoes, or amla with iron-rich meals.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Inhibitors:** Tea and coffee bind dietary iron, making it unavailable. Avoid them before, during, or soon after a meal or when taking IFA supplements.

### Micronutrient: Folic Acid
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Importance:** Essential for red blood cell formation, cell growth, and preventing birth defects of the brain and spinal cord (neural tube defects).
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Timing:** Crucial to consume 3 months before conception and during the first 3 months (first trimester) of pregnancy.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Daily Requirement:** The recommended daily intake is 400 micrograms. A supplement of 500µg (0.5mg) is advised during the first trimester.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Food Sources:** Dark green leafy vegetables (spinach, cabbage), legumes (beans, peas), nuts, liver, citrus fruits (oranges), carrot, ladies finger (okra), and fish.

### Micronutrient: Calcium and Vitamin D
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Importance:** Calcium is essential for the baby’s bone and teeth development, and for preventing high blood pressure and osteoporosis in the mother. Vitamin D is crucial for calcium absorption.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Daily Calcium Requirement (Pregnancy):** Approximately 1000 mg per day.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Daily Calcium Requirement (Adolescent Pregnancy <18 years):** Higher, around 1300 mg per day.
[Target:: BSL | Milestone:: PNC | Action:: Extract] **Daily Calcium Requirement (Postpartum):** Remains at 1000 mg per day, especially while breastfeeding for the first 3 months.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Calcium Food Sources:** Dairy products (milk, cheese, yogurt), green leafy vegetables (spinach, broccoli, dhaniya, methi), legumes (chickpeas, lentils), almonds, mushrooms, boiled eggs, and fish with soft bones (sardines).
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Example Calcium Content:** 1 cup (240 ml) of cow's milk contains approximately 300 mg of calcium.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Vitamin D Requirement:** The daily requirement is 400 IU.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Vitamin D Sources:** Exposure to direct sunlight for at least 15 minutes. Food sources are limited but include fatty fish.

### Micronutrient: Iodine
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Importance:** Mild iodine deficiency can impact the brain development of the unborn child, potentially reducing their Intelligent Quotient (IQ).
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Daily Requirement:** The recommended daily intake is 220 micrograms.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Food Sources:** Iodized salt, milk, curd, fish, boiled eggs, spinach, potato with skin.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Intake Tip:** Use 2 teaspoons of iodized salt daily to meet requirements.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Cooking Tip:** Add iodized salt during the later stages of cooking or sprinkle on food after cooking to minimize iodine loss.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Storage Tip:** Store iodized salt in an airtight glass jar away from hot and humid areas to maintain its iodine content.

### Micronutrient: Vitamin B12
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Importance:** Essential for red blood cell formation, DNA synthesis, and development of the baby’s brain and nervous system. Works with folate to prevent megaloblastic anemia.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Daily Requirement (Pregnant):** 2.6 micrograms/day.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Daily Requirement (Lactating):** 2.8 micrograms/day.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Food Sources:** Primarily animal-based foods like dairy products (milk, curd/yogurt, cheese), eggs, fish (salmon, tuna), meat and poultry (chicken breast, lamb liver), and seafood (crab, prawns).

### Micronutrient: Omega-3 Fatty Acids
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Importance:** Vital for the development of a child’s brain, vision, nervous system, and eyes.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Food Sources:** Fish is an excellent source.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Vegetarian Sources:** Use a combination of mustard oil and rice bran oil or mustard oil and groundnut oil for cooking. Incorporate flax seeds, walnuts, chia seeds, leafy green vegetables (spinach), and beans (kidney beans, soybeans).

### Supplementation Guidelines
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Iron and Folic Acid (IFA) Tablets:** All pregnant women should receive iron tablets, regardless of anemia status.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Prophylactic Dose:** Women with normal HB levels (>11 g/dl) should take 1 IFA tablet daily for 100 days, starting after 14-16 weeks of gestation.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **IFA Composition (Recommendation 1):** Supplementation comprising 60 mg elemental iron and 0.5mg (500 mcg) folic acid is recommended from the 12th week of pregnancy.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **IFA Composition (Recommendation 2):** The IFA program provides tablets with 100 mg elemental iron + 500 mcg folic acid daily for at least 100 days.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Therapeutic Dose (Mild Anemia):** Women with HB < 11 g/dl should take 2 IFA tablets daily for 3 months (200 tablets total).
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **IFA Administration:** Take IFA tablets 2 hours after meals with water to avoid side effects like nausea.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Calcium Carbonate Tablets:** Government provides 360 tablets.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Calcium Administration:** Take twice daily with water, immediately after meals (morning and evening).
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **CRITICAL RULE:** Do not take calcium and IFA tablets together, as they interfere with each other's absorption.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Deworming:** A single Albendazole (400 mg) tablet should be taken during the 4th month (14–16 weeks).

### Foods and Substances to Avoid
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Harmful Substances:** Completely stop consumption of alcohol, tobacco, gutkha, chewing tobacco, bidi, pan supari, applying mishri, and using narcotic drugs.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Beverages:** Avoid tea, coffee, and cola with meals as they inhibit iron absorption. Limit intake of tea or coffee to no more than 2 cups a day. Avoid carbonated beverages.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Unhealthy Foods:** Avoid excessive junk food, processed foods, and foods high in fat, sugar, or salt.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Fats to Avoid:** Avoid foods made with hydrogenated fat (Dalda) and foods containing trans-fat, such as packaged namkeens, chips, and cakes.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Food Safety Risks:** Do not consume raw or half-boiled eggs or unboiled (unpasteurized) milk.

### Food Safety and Cooking Tips
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Handwashing:** Wash hands with soap and water before cooking or eating.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Produce Washing:** Soak all vegetables and fruits in salt water for some time, rinse and wash them thoroughly before peeling and cutting.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Utensil Cleaning:** Wash all utensils before use. You can use one tsp of household bleach in 1 liter of water to clean them, then rinse well.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Cooking:** Cook food properly before eating. Reheat leftover food well before eating it.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Oil Usage:** Do not re-use or overheat oils.
[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **Food Storage:** Do not keep raw or cooked food at room temperature for longer than 2 hours. Do not keep cooked food in the fridge for more than 2 days.

[Target:: Pregnancy Nutrition | Milestone:: Dietary Guidelines | Action:: Extract] **A:** You should not take iron (IFA) and calcium tablets together because they interfere with each other's absorption. Calcium can bind with iron in your digestive system, reducing the amount of iron your body can absorb. It is recommended to take them at different meals, for example, calcium immediately after meals and iron two hours after meals.
