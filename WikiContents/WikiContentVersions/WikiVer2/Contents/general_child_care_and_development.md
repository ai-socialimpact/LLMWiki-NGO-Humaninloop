## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer
<executive_brief_block>
### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: Child Care | Milestone:: General (0-5y) | Action:: Guideline] This document provides a comprehensive overview of non-nutritional and non-medical child care practices for children from birth to five years of age. For newborns, it emphasizes delaying the first bath for at least 24 hours, keeping the baby warm, ensuring the umbilical cord is kept dry without applying any substances, and practicing skin-to-skin contact. General hygiene practices for all children under five include regular handwashing with soap and water, using clean water, and maintaining a clean home environment. Home safety is a critical component, with guidelines on storing toxic substances out of reach, securing open water sources like pools and wells, keeping the home free of smoke, and protecting children from hazards like sharp objects and hot surfaces. The document outlines age-appropriate sleep requirements, recommending 14-17 hours for infants up to 3 months, 12-16 hours for infants 4-11 months, 11-14 hours for toddlers 1-2 years, and 10-13 hours for children over 2. It stresses the importance of placing babies on their backs to sleep to reduce risks. For development, it encourages talking to children, engaging in play, and promoting physical activity. Specific activity guidelines are at least 30 minutes daily for infants and 180 minutes throughout the day for older children. Screen time should be avoided for children under two and limited to 60 minutes for older children. The guide also details Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) as a method for low birth weight infants, involving prolonged skin-to-skin contact and exclusive breastfeeding to stabilize temperature, prevent infection, and promote bonding.

**Question:** How can I prevent drowning at home?
[Target:: Child Care | Milestone:: General (0-5y) | Action:: Safety]

</qa_pair>

**Question:** Who can provide Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC)?
[Target:: Child Care | Milestone:: Newborn (0-28d) | Action:: Guideline]
**Answer:** KMC can be provided by mothers, fathers, and other adult family members. The provider must be willing, in good health, free from serious illness, and maintain good personal hygiene.
</qa_pair>
<qa_pair>
[Target:: Child Care | Milestone:: Newborn (0-28d) | Action:: Guideline]
**Question:** Is skin-to-skin contact only for low birth weight babies?
[Target:: Child Care | Milestone:: Newborn (0-28d) | Action:: Guideline]
**Answer:** No. While Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) is a specific, prolonged practice for stable low birth weight infants, the World Health Organization (WHO) recommends skin-to-skin care immediately after delivery for every newborn, regardless of birth weight, to ensure warmth and help with early breastfeeding.
</qa_pair>
