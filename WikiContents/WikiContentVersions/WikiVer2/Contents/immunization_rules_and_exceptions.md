## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer
<executive_brief_block>
### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] This document provides a detailed guide to handling exceptions and deviations from the standard immunization schedule as per India's Universal Immunization Programme (UIP). A core principle is that a delayed start or interruption in a multi-dose vaccine series does not require restarting the schedule; the series should be picked up where it was left off. The document specifies strict upper age limits for several vaccines: BCG is not given beyond one year of age, OPV up to five years, DPT up to seven years, and JE up to fifteen years. For Pentavalent, IPV, PCV, and Rotavirus vaccines, the series can only be initiated if the first dose is given before the child turns one year old. If initiated before one year, the series can be completed regardless of the child's age. Specific time limits apply to newborn vaccines: the Hepatitis B birth dose must be given within 24 hours for efficacy against maternal transmission, and the OPV zero dose within 15 days. The guidelines also clarify that mild illnesses, such as a cough, cold, mild fever, or mild diarrhea, are not contraindications for vaccination. However, vaccination should be postponed for children with moderate to severe illness requiring hospitalization until their condition improves. Oral vaccines should be deferred in cases of severe diarrhea or recurrent vomiting until the condition is controlled.

### D. Important Facts Summary
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Do Not Restart Series:** If a child is late for a subsequent dose of a multi-dose vaccine, do not restart the series. Administer the next due dose and continue the schedule.
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Upper Age Limits:** BCG: 1 year; OPV: 5 years; Measles/MR: 5 years; DPT: 7 years; JE: 15 years.
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **One-Year Rule for Specific Vaccines:** Pentavalent, IPV, PCV, and Rotavirus vaccines cannot be started if the child is already over one year of age. If the first dose was given before one year, the series can be completed.
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Sick Child Vaccination:** Mild illnesses (cough, cold, mild fever, mild diarrhea) are NOT contraindications. Vaccination can proceed.
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Contraindications for Severe Illness:** Postpone vaccination for children with moderate to severe illness requiring hospitalization (e.g., high-grade fever, severe diarrhea) until they recover.
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Oral Vaccines and GI Issues:** Oral vaccines can be given to a child with mild diarrhea. They should be postponed in cases of severe diarrhea or recurrent vomiting until the condition is controlled.
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Newborn Vaccine Deadlines:** Hepatitis B birth dose must be given within 24 hours of birth. OPV zero dose must be given within 15 days of birth.
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Simultaneous Vaccination:** It is safe and effective to give all due vaccines (e.g., BCG, penta, OPV, IPV, MR) during the same session to a previously unvaccinated 9-month-old child.

### F. Top 5 to 10 Most Popular FAQs
<qa_pair>
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Question:** If a child is brought late for a subsequent dose of multi-dose vaccines (for example, after more than the recommended one month), should the health worker restart the series from the first dose?
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Answer:** No. There is no need to restart the series or schedule again in case a child has received some doses of scheduled vaccines but is brought late for the subsequent doses. If the child is brought late, you should give the next dose of the vaccine and motivate the parents to bring the child for the remaining doses at the recommended interval as per the immunization schedule.
</qa_pair>
<qa_pair>
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Question:** What are the upper age limits for various vaccines?
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Answer:** According to the National Immunization Schedule, the upper age limits are: BCG: up to one year of age; OPV: up to five years; Measles/MR: up to five years; DPT: up to 7 years; JE: up to 15 years. For pentavalent, IPV, PCV and Rotavirus vaccines, if at least one dose is given before one year of age, then remaining doses can be administered. If the first dose is not administered before one year of age, then these vaccines cannot be administered to the child under UIP.
</qa_pair>
<qa_pair>
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Question:** If a child is sick, can she/he be vaccinated?
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Answer:** A sick child suffering from mild illness (like cough, cold, or mild fever), mild diarrhoea or vomiting can be safely vaccinated with injectable or oral vaccines. However, a child who has some serious illness or is hospitalized (like in high grade fever, severe diarrhoea, etc.), should not be vaccinated until his or her condition improves.
</qa_pair>
<qa_pair>
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Question:** Can vaccines, especially oral vaccines, be administered to a child with diarrhoea and vomiting?
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Answer:** Oral vaccines can be given to a child with mild diarrhoea. However, if a child has severe diarrhoea requiring hospitalization, oral vaccines should be given only after diarrhoea is controlled. Oral vaccines should not be given to a child who is vomiting recurrently until vomiting is controlled.
</qa_pair>
<qa_pair>
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Question:** If a child who has never been vaccinated is brought in at 9 completed months but before 12 completed months of age, then, can all the due vaccines be given to a child on the same day?
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Answer:** Yes, all the due vaccines can be given during the same session but at recommended injection sites, using separate AD syringes. It is safe and effective to give BCG, penta, OPV, IPV, MR, RVV (where applicable), PCV (where applicable) JE (where applicable) vaccines and Vitamin A at the same time to a 9-month-old child who has never been vaccinated. If more than one injection has to be given in one limb then ensure that the distance between the two injection sites is at least 1 inch apart.
</qa_pair>
<qa_pair>
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Question:** Which vaccines can be given to a child between 1 and 5 years of age who has never been vaccinated?
[Target:: Immunization Rules | Milestone:: Catch-up & Exceptions | Action:: Extract] **Answer:** Such a child will not receive BCG, Hepatitis B, Rotavirus, Penta and IPV. Give DPT1, OPV1, measles 1, JE 1 (where applicable) and 2 ml of Vitamin A solution. Then follow with the second and third doses of DPT and OPV at 1-month intervals. Give Measles 2 as per the schedule /1 month later. Give a booster dose of OPV/DPT at a minimum of 6 months after administering OPV 3/DPT 3. Also give Vit A at a 6 months interval till 5 years of age. For an unvaccinated child more than 16 months of age, the interval between Measles 1 and Measles 2 is 4 weeks and for JE 1 and JE 2 (where applicable) the interval is 3 months.
</qa_pair>
