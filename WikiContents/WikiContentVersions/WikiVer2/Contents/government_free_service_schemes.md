## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer
<executive_brief_block>
### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: Government Schemes | Milestone:: Free Services | Action:: Extraction] This document provides a comprehensive overview of free services offered under various Indian government health schemes, primarily focusing on maternal and child health. The cornerstone scheme, Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK), ensures that pregnant women receive completely free services at government health institutions. This includes free normal and caesarean deliveries, all necessary medicines and materials, diagnostic tests like blood tests, urine tests, and sonography, and nutritional meals during their stay. JSSK also covers free transport from home to the hospital, between facilities if a referral is needed, and back home after discharge. The program guarantees a free stay of 3 days for normal delivery and 7 days for caesarean delivery. For sick infants under one year of age, JSSK provides free treatment, medicines, diagnostics, and transport, with a complete exemption from all hospital-related charges.
[Target:: Government Schemes | Milestone:: Free Services | Action:: Extraction] In addition to delivery and infant care, the government provides extensive free antenatal and immunization services. Under the Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA), all pregnant women are entitled to free, comprehensive antenatal care on the 9th of every month. Essential supplements, including 180 Iron-Folic Acid tablets and 360 Calcium Carbonate tablets, are provided free of cost at government health centers. Pregnant women also receive two mandatory, free Tetanus-Diphtheria (T.D.) injections. The Universal Immunization Programme (UIP) offers all scheduled vaccines for infants and children free of cost, including BCG, Polio (OPV), Hepatitis B, Pentavalent, Rotavirus, and Measles/MR, along with the necessary syringes and the Mother and Child Protection card.
