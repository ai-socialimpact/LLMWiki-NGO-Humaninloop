## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer
<executive_brief_block>
### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Overview | Action:: Summarize] This document provides a comprehensive guide to standard antenatal care (ANC) for pregnant women, detailing the procedural timeline and essential health practices from conception through to birth preparedness. The process begins with early pregnancy registration, ideally within the first 12 weeks, facilitated by healthcare workers like ANMs and ASHAs. A structured schedule of at least four antenatal check-ups is outlined: the first within 12 weeks, the second between 16-24 weeks, the third from 28-32 weeks, and the fourth at 36-38 weeks. Each visit involves key examinations such as weight monitoring, blood pressure checks, and assessment of the baby's growth. A series of routine tests are conducted throughout the pregnancy, including urine tests for sugar and protein, and comprehensive blood tests to screen for anemia (CBC), blood group, Rh compatibility, gestational diabetes, HIV, syphilis (VDRL), and other conditions. Ultrasounds are scheduled at specific intervals (6-9 weeks, 18-22 weeks, 28-32 weeks) to monitor fetal development. The guide also specifies the provision and intake of essential government-provided supplements, including 180 Iron-Folic Acid (IFA) tablets and 360 Calcium Carbonate tablets, along with a single deworming (Albendazole) tablet. Immunization protocols, specifically two doses of the Tetanus-Diphtheria (T.D.) vaccine, are detailed. Crucially, the document lists critical danger signs that require immediate medical attention, such as severe headaches, bleeding, or reduced fetal movement. Finally, it provides a checklist for birth preparedness, covering the selection of a birth companion, packing an essential delivery bag, arranging finances and emergency transport, and ensuring emotional support for the mother.

### D. Important Facts Summary
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Overview | Action:: Highlight] **Pregnancy Registration:** Register the pregnancy within the first 12 weeks.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Overview | Action:: Highlight] **Antenatal Check-ups:** A minimum of four check-ups are required: 1st (up to 12 weeks), 2nd (16-24 weeks), 3rd (28-32 weeks), and 4th (36-38 weeks).
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Overview | Action:: Highlight] **Key Examinations:** Regular monitoring includes weight, blood pressure, and the baby's growth and position.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Overview | Action:: Highlight] **Essential Supplements:** Government provides 180 Iron-Folic Acid (IFA) tablets and 360 Calcium Carbonate tablets.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Overview | Action:: Highlight] **Immunization:** Two mandatory doses of the Tetanus-Diphtheria (T.D.) vaccine are required. The first is given upon pregnancy confirmation, and the second is 4-6 weeks later.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Overview | Action:: Highlight] **Deworming:** A single 400 mg Albendazole tablet should be taken during the 4th month (14–16 weeks).
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Overview | Action:: Highlight] **Ultrasound Schedule:** Three key ultrasounds are recommended: at 6-9 weeks (viability), 18-22 weeks (anomaly scan), and 28-32 weeks (growth scan).
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Overview | Action:: Highlight] **Danger Signs:** Immediately consult a doctor for symptoms like severe headaches, vaginal bleeding, swelling of hands/face/feet, or reduced baby movement.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Overview | Action:: Highlight] **Birth Preparedness:** Plan for delivery by choosing a companion, preparing a delivery bag, arranging funds, and identifying emergency transport (Ambulance: 102/108).
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Overview | Action:: Highlight] **Supplement Intake Rule:** Do not take calcium and IFA tablets together as it interferes with absorption.

## 3. Exhaustive List of Facts
<facts_block>
### **Pregnancy Registration**
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Registration | Action:: Guideline] If you have missed your period, visit the nearest health post to confirm your pregnancy.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Registration | Action:: Guideline] Every pregnancy should be registered within the first 12 weeks to receive timely and appropriate care.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Registration | Action:: Guideline] Registration can be done at the nearest government hospital or healthcare centre.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Registration | Action:: Guideline] The ANM (Auxiliary Nurse Midwife) is the primary point of contact for registration.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Registration | Action:: Guideline] Village Health and Nutrition Day (VHND) at an Anganwadi centre can be used for early registration and check-ups.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Registration | Action:: Guideline] Assistance for registration can be sought from ASHAs (Accredited Social Health Activists), Anganwadi Workers (AWWs), and other community members.

### **Antenatal Check-up (ANC) Schedule**
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Schedule] Every pregnant woman should visit a healthcare facility for routine check-ups at least once every three months.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Schedule] A minimum of four antenatal checkups (ANC) are recommended.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Schedule] **1st check-up:** Should be conducted after missing a period or within the first 3 months (up to 12 weeks).
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Schedule] **2nd check-up:** Scheduled between the 4th and 6th month (16 to 24 weeks) of pregnancy.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Schedule] **3rd check-up:** Scheduled during the 7th and 8th month (28 to 32 weeks) of pregnancy.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Schedule] **4th check-up:** Scheduled in the 9th month (36 to 38 weeks).

### **Examinations During Antenatal Check-ups**
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Examination] **Weight monitoring:** A pregnant woman's weight is regularly monitored.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Examination] **Blood pressure check:** Blood pressure is checked at appointments.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Examination] **Baby’s growth and position:** The baby's development and position are regularly monitored.

### **Routine Tests During Pregnancy**
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Test] **Urine tests:** Performed to check for sugar and protein levels, detect urinary tract infections, or identify proteinuria.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Test] **Blood tests:** A series of blood tests are conducted throughout pregnancy.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Test] **Blood Group and Rh Compatibility:** Done to detect Rh incompatibility risks.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Test] **Complete Blood Count (CBC):** Checks for anaemia, infection, and platelet levels. An initial CBC is done in early pregnancy, repeated between 14–27 weeks, and again between 28–32 weeks.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Test] **Hemoglobin (HB) testing:** Anemia is diagnosed using a blood test measuring hemoglobin levels. This can be done with portable hemoglobin meters (finger-prick) or a CBC test (from a vein).
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Test] **Blood Sugar levels:** Fasting, postprandial, or HbA1c tests are done to identify gestational or pre-existing diabetes.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Test] **HIV testing:** Mandatory to prevent vertical transmission.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Test] **VDRL (Venereal Disease Research Laboratory) test:** Screens for syphilis.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Test] **Hepatitis B & C screening:** To detect and manage HBV or HCV infections.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Test] **TSH (Thyroid-Stimulating Hormone):** To detect thyroid disorders that can affect fetal growth.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Test] **Rubella IgG and IgM:** To check for immunity against rubella or active infection.

### **Ultrasound (Sonography) Schedule**
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Ultrasound] **6–9 Weeks:** To confirm gestational age, fetal viability, and rule out ectopic pregnancy.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Ultrasound] **18–22 Weeks:** To check for fetal structural abnormalities (anomaly scan).
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: ANC | Action:: Ultrasound] **28–32 Weeks:** To assess fetal growth, amniotic fluid levels, and placental health.

### **Essential Medications and Supplements**
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Supplementation | Action:: Guideline] Supplements like folic acid, iron, and calcium are prescribed once pregnancy is confirmed.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Supplementation | Action:: Guideline] Government provides 180 Iron-Folic Acid (IFA) tablets to prevent anemia.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Supplementation | Action:: Guideline] Government provides 360 Calcium Carbonate tablets to prevent high blood pressure and support development.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Supplementation | Action:: Guideline] **Iron and Folic Acid (IFA) tablets:** Take 2 hours after meals with water to avoid side effects like nausea.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Supplementation | Action:: Guideline] **Calcium Carbonate tablets:** Take twice daily with water, immediately after meals (morning and evening).
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Supplementation | Action:: Guideline] Do not take calcium and IFA tablets together, as they can interfere with absorption.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Supplementation | Action:: Guideline] **Albendazole (400 mg):** A single deworming tablet should be taken during the 4th month (14–16 weeks).
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Supplementation | Action:: Guideline] These supplements are available free of cost at sub-centres, health centres, or health posts.

### **Immunization During Pregnancy**
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Immunization | Action:: Guideline] Two doses of Tetanus-Diphtheria (T.D.) Injections are mandatory during pregnancy.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Immunization | Action:: Guideline] **First dose:** Administered as soon as pregnancy is confirmed.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Immunization | Action:: Guideline] **Second dose:** Administered 4–6 weeks after the first dose.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Immunization | Action:: Guideline] If a woman received two TT doses in the last 3 years, she can take a booster dose instead.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Immunization | Action:: Guideline] The T.D. vaccine is safe during pregnancy and does not harm the baby.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Immunization | Action:: Guideline] The injection site may become slightly swollen, red, and painful, but these effects subside within a few days.

### **Warning / Danger Signs During Pregnancy**
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: DangerSigns | Action:: Identify] Consult a healthcare provider immediately if any of the following symptoms occur:
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: DangerSigns | Action:: Identify] Severe headaches or dizziness.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: DangerSigns | Action:: Identify] Swelling of hands, face, or feet.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: DangerSigns | Action:: Identify] Bleeding or fluid discharge from the vagina (any colour).
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: DangerSigns | Action:: Identify] Severe abdominal pain or persistent backache.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: DangerSigns | Action:: Identify] Fever or chills.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: DangerSigns | Action:: Identify] Fainting or extreme weakness.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: DangerSigns | Action:: Identify] Reduced or no movement of the baby.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: DangerSigns | Action:: Identify] Night blindness.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: DangerSigns | Action:: Identify] Severe anaemia (common signs include a pale tongue, tiredness, and general body swelling).

### **Hygiene, Rest, and Lifestyle**
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Lifestyle | Action:: Guideline] **Handwashing:** Wash hands with soap and water before eating and after using the toilet.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Lifestyle | Action:: Guideline] **Clothing:** Wear clean and comfortable clothing; avoid tight garments.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Lifestyle | Action:: Guideline] **Oral health:** Brush teeth twice daily for at least two minutes. Get regular dental check-ups as gum disease can increase the risk of preterm low birth weight babies.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Lifestyle | Action:: Guideline] **Sleep and Rest:** Get at least 8 hours of sleep at night and 2 hours of rest during the day.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Lifestyle | Action:: Guideline] **Sleep position:** Prefer lying on your left side as it improves blood flow to the fetus. In the third trimester, lie on your side with slightly bent knees.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Lifestyle | Action:: Guideline] **Avoid overexertion:** Avoid heavy work, such as lifting heavy objects.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Lifestyle | Action:: Guideline] **Substance Avoidance:** Completely stop consumption of alcohol, tobacco, gutkha, bidi, pan supari, and narcotic drugs.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Lifestyle | Action:: Guideline] **Precautions:** Avoid tasks near stoves or fire. Allow pregnancy to reach the full term of 39 weeks. Avoid keeping a mobile phone near your stomach and prefer using speaker mode.

### **Birth Preparedness Plan**
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: BirthPreparedness | Action:: Plan] A birth preparedness plan involves active family participation to ensure a safe delivery.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: BirthPreparedness | Action:: Plan] **Selecting a supportive companion:** Choose a person (family member or friend) to provide emotional, physical, and practical support during labor and delivery.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: BirthPreparedness | Action:: Plan] **Essential delivery bag preparation:** Prepare a bag with necessary essentials to avoid a last-minute rush.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: BirthPreparedness | Action:: Plan] **Bag Contents - Documents:** Aadhaar card, maternal child protection card, all blood reports, and ultrasound reports.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: BirthPreparedness | Action:: Plan] **Bag Contents - Hygiene items:** Soap, towel, toothbrush.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: BirthPreparedness | Action:: Plan] **Bag Contents - Clothing:** Clothes for the new-born and the mother.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: BirthPreparedness | Action:: Plan] **Timely healthcare access:** Contact the healthcare centre immediately if labor begins or complications arise.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: BirthPreparedness | Action:: Plan] **Financial and emergency preparedness:** Set aside sufficient funds for emergencies.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: BirthPreparedness | Action:: Plan] **Emergency Transport:** Utilize government ambulance services available toll-free at 102/108.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: BirthPreparedness | Action:: Plan] **Emergency Blood:** Identify a family member or friend willing to donate blood in emergencies.
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: BirthPreparedness | Action:: Plan] **Emotional and social support:** Support from the husband, mother-in-law, and other family members is crucial.

[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Supplementation | Action:: Query] Why shouldn't I take my iron and calcium tablets at the same time?
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Supplementation | Action:: Answer] You should not take calcium and Iron-Folic Acid (IFA) tablets together because calcium can interfere with the body's ability to absorb iron, making the iron supplement less effective. It is recommended to take them at different times of the day.
</qa_pair>
<qa_pair>
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Immunization | Action:: Query] Is it normal for the Tetanus (T.D.) injection site to be sore?
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Immunization | Action:: Answer] Yes, it is a normal reaction for the injection site to become slightly swollen, red, and painful after a Tetanus-Diphtheria (T.D.) vaccine. These effects typically subside on their own within a few days.
</qa_pair>
<qa_pair>
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: BirthPreparedness | Action:: Query] What documents should I pack in my hospital delivery bag?
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: BirthPreparedness | Action:: Answer] Your essential documents for the hospital bag include your Aadhaar card, the maternal child protection (MCP) card, all of your blood test reports, and all of your ultrasound (sonography) reports.
</qa_pair>
<qa_pair>
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Lifestyle | Action:: Query] What is the best sleeping position during pregnancy?
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Lifestyle | Action:: Answer] It is best to sleep on your side, preferably your left side, as this position improves blood circulation to the fetus. In the third trimester, lying on your side with your knees slightly bent is recommended for maximum comfort.
</qa_pair>
<qa_pair>
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Supplementation | Action:: Query] When should I take the deworming tablet?
[Target:: PregnancyCare | Milestone:: Supplementation | Action:: Answer] A single deworming tablet, Albendazole (400 mg), should be taken during the fourth month of pregnancy, which is between 14 and 16 weeks of gestation.
</qa_pair>
