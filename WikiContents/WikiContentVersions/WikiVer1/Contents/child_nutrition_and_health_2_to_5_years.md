## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer
<executive_brief_block>
### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: child_health_2_5_years | Milestone:: overview | Scope:: summary] This document provides comprehensive health and nutrition guidelines for children aged 2 to 5 years. Key recommendations include ensuring children are physically active for at least 180 minutes per day and limiting sedentary screen time to a maximum of 60 minutes daily. Adequate sleep is crucial, with 11-14 hours recommended for 2-year-olds and 10-13 hours for children aged 3-5. Dietary guidance emphasizes providing a variety of foods, including meat, poultry, fish, fruits, vegetables, and eggs, while encouraging children to eat without forcing them. The guide also covers essential safety and hygiene practices, such as hand washing, maintaining a smoke-free environment, and securing the home against hazards like toxic substances and open water. Furthermore, it includes detailed WHO data on average weight and height for this age group to monitor growth, alongside definitions and risks associated with malnutrition, including stunting, wasting, overweight, and underweight conditions. Parents and caregivers are advised to monitor for danger signs like poor eating or reduced activity and to seek timely health services.

[Target:: child_health_2_5_years | Milestone:: scope | Scope:: boundaries] Strict Out-of-Scope Exclusions: This container is FORBIDDEN from containing information on infant feeding (under 2 years), complementary feeding, breastfeeding promotion, Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC), low birth weight (LBW) infant care, and any growth data for children under 24 months of age.

### C. Contrastive Boundary Analysis (What This Document Is NOT)
[Target:: child_health_2_5_years | Milestone:: infant_care | Context:: temporal_contrast] AGE CONTRAST (vs. Infants <2 Years): This document's guidelines are distinct from infant care. For example, while this guide limits screen time to 60 minutes for children 2-5 years, screen time should be avoided entirely for children under 2. Similarly, sleep duration recommendations of 10-14 hours for preschoolers differ from the 12-17 hours required for infants. Infant-specific protocols like KMC and exclusive breastfeeding are strictly out of scope.
[Target:: child_health_2_5_years | Milestone:: malnutrition | Context:: intervention_contrast] CONDITION CONTRAST (vs. Acute Illness): The guidelines herein are for routine health maintenance and growth monitoring. They are not a substitute for clinical care. If a child exhibits danger signs such as not eating or drinking, being less active than usual, or showing symptoms of acute malnutrition (wasting), immediate consultation with a health worker is required. These protocols do not cover the management of severe acute malnutrition or other medical emergencies.

### Malnutrition Definitions (WHO)
[Target:: child_health_2_5_years | Milestone:: malnutrition | Action:: definition] Stunting is the result of long-term nutritional deprivation from poor diets or recurrent infections, leading to growth retardation. (Source: ANTHROPOMETRY MEASURES OF CHILD KB.md)
[Target:: child_health_2_5_years | Milestone:: malnutrition | Action:: risk] Stunting can result in delayed mental development, poor school performance, and reduced intellectual capacity. (Source: ANTHROPOMETRY MEASURES OF CHILD KB.md)
[Target:: child_health_2_5_years | Milestone:: malnutrition | Action:: definition] Wasting is a symptom of acute undernutrition, usually from insufficient food intake or a high incidence of infectious diseases like diarrhoea. (Source: ANTHROPOMETRY MEASURES OF CHILD KB.md)
[Target:: child_health_2_5_years | Milestone:: malnutrition | Action:: risk] Wasting impairs the immune system, leading to increased severity, duration, and susceptibility to infectious diseases, and an increased risk of death. (Source: ANTHROPOMETRY MEASURES OF CHILD KB.md)
[Target:: child_health_2_5_years | Milestone:: malnutrition | Action:: definition] Underweight is an indicator based on weight-for-age, and even mild underweight status increases mortality risk in children. (Source: ANTHROPOMETRY MEASURES OF CHILD KB.md)
[Target:: child_health_2_5_years | Milestone:: malnutrition | Action:: definition] Childhood obesity is associated with a higher probability of obesity in adulthood. (Source: ANTHROPOMETRY MEASURES OF CHILD KB.md)
[Target:: child_health_2_5_years | Milestone:: malnutrition | Action:: risk] Health consequences of childhood obesity include cardiovascular diseases (heart disease, stroke), diabetes, musculoskeletal disorders (osteoarthritis), and cancers (endometrium, breast, colon). (Source: ANTHROPOMETRY MEASURES OF CHILD KB.md)
