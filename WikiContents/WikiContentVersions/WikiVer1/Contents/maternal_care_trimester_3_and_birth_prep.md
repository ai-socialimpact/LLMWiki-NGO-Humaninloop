## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer

### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: trimester3_birth_prep | Milestone:: week_29_delivery | Scope:: overview] This document outlines comprehensive care protocols for pregnant women from week 29 until delivery, covering the third trimester and birth preparation. It specifies the schedule for the final two antenatal care (ANC) visits: ANC 3 between weeks 28-32 and ANC 4 between weeks 36-38. Key health monitoring during this period includes tracking the baby's growth and position, blood pressure, and hemoglobin levels to manage anemia. Nutritional guidance emphasizes an increased intake of an additional 350 calories and 18 grams of protein daily during the third trimester, alongside continued Iron and Folic Acid (IFA) supplementation. A central focus is the Birth Preparedness and Complication Readiness (BPCR) plan, which involves preparing a delivery bag with essential documents and items, arranging for emergency transport using toll-free ambulance services (102/108), setting aside funds, and identifying a potential blood donor. The document lists critical danger signs requiring immediate medical attention, such as severe headaches, vaginal bleeding, or reduced fetal movement, and details government schemes like Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK) that provide free delivery services and transport.

### Anemia Management Protocols
[Target:: trimester3_birth_prep | Milestone:: anemia_management | Condition:: mild_anemia] For Hb levels of 9–11 gm/dl, the treatment is 2 IFA tablets daily (morning and evening) for at least 100 days. (Source: PNC nutrition and anemia.md)
[Target:: trimester3_birth_prep | Milestone:: anemia_management | Condition:: moderate_anemia] For Hb levels of 7–9 gm/dl, the cause should be investigated. For cases in the third trimester, referral to a First Referral Unit (FRU) or Medical College (MC) is required. (Source: PNC nutrition and anemia.md)
[Target:: trimester3_birth_prep | Milestone:: anemia_management | Condition:: moderate_anemia] For Hb levels of 7–8 gm/dl, injectable iron (parenteral) with oral folic acid may be administered. (Source: PNC nutrition and anemia.md)
[Target:: trimester3_birth_prep | Milestone:: anemia_management | Condition:: severe_anemia] For Hb levels below 7 gm/dl, management occurs at an FRU, District Hospital (DH), or Medical College (MC) and includes parenteral iron therapy. (Source: PNC nutrition and anemia.md)
[Target:: trimester3_birth_prep | Milestone:: anemia_management | Condition:: critical_anemia] For Hb levels below 5 gm/dl, immediate hospitalisation for intensive care and possible blood transfusion is required. (Source: PNC nutrition and anemia.md)

### Government Schemes for Delivery
[Target:: trimester3_birth_prep | Milestone:: government_schemes | Program:: jssk] Under the Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK), pregnant mothers receive free delivery services, including normal and caesarean delivery, at government health institutions. (Source: Government Schemes in Maternal Child Health.md)
[Target:: trimester3_birth_prep | Milestone:: government_schemes | Program:: jssk] JSSK provides free transport from home to the hospital for delivery, transfer to another hospital if required, and transportation back home. (Source: Government Schemes in Maternal Child Health.md)
[Target:: trimester3_birth_prep | Milestone:: government_schemes | Program:: jssk] Under JSSK, pregnant women receive free medicines, diagnostic tests (blood, urine, sonography), and nutritional meals during their hospital stay. (Source: Government Schemes in Maternal Child Health.md)
[Target:: trimester3_birth_prep | Milestone:: government_schemes | Program:: jsy] Under Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY), pregnant women from BPL families or Scheduled Castes/Tribes who deliver in a government hospital can receive a financial benefit for up to two live births. (Source: Janani Suraksha Yojana.md)
[Target:: trimester3_birth_prep | Milestone:: government_schemes | Program:: jsy] The JSY benefit in rural areas of Maharashtra is ₹700, and in urban areas, it is ₹600, deposited into the woman's bank account. (Source: Janani Suraksha Yojana.md)
