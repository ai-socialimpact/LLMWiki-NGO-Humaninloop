## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer

### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: jsy | Milestone:: scheme_overview | Action:: summary] The Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY) is a government scheme designed to promote institutional deliveries in government hospitals to reduce maternal and neonatal mortality. The scheme provides a direct cash benefit to eligible pregnant women for up to two live births. Eligibility is restricted to women from Below Poverty Line (BPL) families or those belonging to Scheduled Castes (SC) and Scheduled Tribes (ST). In Maharashtra, the cash assistance is ₹700 for rural areas and ₹600 for urban areas, deposited directly into the beneficiary's bank account. To avail the benefit, the pregnant woman must contact a government health facility with the help of an ASHA worker. A specific set of documents, including proof of BPL/caste status, identity, address, bank details, and a delivery certificate from the government hospital, is required. The payment is processed within 7 days of delivery, provided the necessary documents were submitted during pregnancy registration or subsequent checkups.
