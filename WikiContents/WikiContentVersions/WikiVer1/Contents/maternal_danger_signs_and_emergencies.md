## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer
<executive_brief_block>
### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: maternal_emergency | Milestone:: danger_sign_recognition | Action:: overview] This document provides a critical checklist of maternal danger signs during pregnancy that signal a potential emergency and require immediate consultation with a healthcare provider. The key warning signs include severe headaches or dizziness, swelling of the hands, face, or feet, any bleeding or fluid discharge from the vagina, severe abdominal pain or persistent backache, fever or chills, fainting or extreme weakness, and reduced or no fetal movement. Additionally, night blindness and severe anemia are identified as danger signs. Severe anemia is clinically defined by a hemoglobin (Hb) level below 7 g/dl and presents with symptoms such as severe weakness, giddiness, breathlessness, pallor, a pale tongue, and general body swelling. Any pregnant woman exhibiting these symptoms, particularly a confirmed Hb level below 7 g/dl, must be referred to a health facility immediately. Cases with Hb below 5 g/dl may require immediate hospitalization for intensive care, including potential blood transfusions.

### C. Contrastive Boundary Analysis (What This Document Is NOT)
[Target:: maternal_emergency | Severity:: moderate_anemia | Context:: intervention_contrast] SEVERITY CONTRAST: Contrast routine anemia management against emergency triggers. While moderate anemia (Hb 9-11 g/dl) is managed with oral IFA tablets at the sub-centre level, severe anemia (Hb < 7 g/dl) is a critical emergency requiring immediate referral to a higher facility (PHC/CHC/FRU) for potential parenteral iron, intensive care, or blood transfusion. Do not apply routine oral protocols to severe emergency cases.
[Target:: maternal_emergency | Symptom:: routine_vs_emergency | Context:: symptom_contrast] SYMPTOM CONTRAST: Contrast common pregnancy discomforts with emergency danger signs. While mild headaches or backaches can be normal, a "severe headache" or "persistent backache" are listed as danger signs requiring immediate medical consultation. Similarly, while some swelling can occur, swelling of the "hands, face, or feet" is a specific warning sign.
