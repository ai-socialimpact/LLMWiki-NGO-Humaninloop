## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer
<executive_brief_block>
### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: ebf | Milestone:: 0_6_months | Scope:: overview] For the first 6 months of life, exclusive breastfeeding is the optimal source of nutrition, providing all the nutrients, including water, that an infant needs for healthy growth and development. This means the infant receives only breast milk and no other foods, drinks, or even water, although prescribed medicines and vitamins are permissible. Breastfeeding should be initiated as early as possible after birth, ideally within the first hour, to provide the baby with colostrum, the nutrient-rich first milk that acts as the baby's first immunization. Feeding should be on-demand, which typically means 8 to 12 times in a 24-hour period, guided by the baby's hunger cues rather than a strict schedule. Adequacy of milk intake can be reliably monitored by ensuring the baby has more than six wet diapers per day and shows appropriate weight gain (20-30g per day for a full-term baby). Exclusive breastfeeding offers significant benefits for both the infant, including protection against infections and allergies, and the mother, such as promoting postpartum recovery and fostering a strong emotional bond.

## 4. Granular Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)
<secondary_faqs_block>
<qa_pair>
[Target:: ebf | Milestone:: 0_6_months | Query:: adequacy_faq] Question: What are the signs that my baby is getting enough milk?
[Target:: ebf | Milestone:: 0_6_months | Query:: adequacy_faq] Answer: The two most reliable signs are adequate urine output (passing urine more than six times in 24 hours) and steady weight gain (about 20-30 grams per day for a full-term baby).
</qa_pair>
<qa_pair>
[Target:: ebf | Milestone:: birth_initiation | Query:: colostrum_faq] Question: What is colostrum?
[Target:: ebf | Milestone:: birth_initiation | Query:: colostrum_faq] Answer: Colostrum is the thick, yellowish first milk produced in the first few days after delivery. It is highly nutritious, rich in Vitamin A, and contains antibodies that protect your baby from illness, acting like a first vaccination.
</qa_pair>
<qa_pair>
[Target:: ebf | Milestone:: 0_6_months | Query:: maternal_illness_faq] Question: Can I breastfeed my baby if I have a cold or fever?
[Target:: ebf | Milestone:: 0_6_months | Query:: maternal_illness_faq] Answer: Yes, it is safe to continue breastfeeding. Common viral infections like a cold or cough are not transmitted through breast milk. In fact, your body produces antibodies that are passed to your baby through the milk, which helps protect them. Remember to wash your hands and wear a mask.
</qa_pair>
<qa_pair>
[Target:: ebf | Milestone:: 0_6_months | Query:: c_section_faq] Question: Is it possible to breastfeed after a C-section?
[Target:: ebf | Milestone:: 0_6_months | Query:: c_section_faq] Answer: Yes, absolutely. You can begin breastfeeding shortly after birth with assistance from a nurse. Positions like the side-lying or football hold can help you feed comfortably without putting pressure on your incision.
</qa_pair>
<qa_pair>
[Target:: ebf | Milestone:: 0_6_months | Query:: bottle_feeding_faq] Question: Why shouldn't I use a feeding bottle for expressed milk?
[Target:: ebf | Milestone:: 0_6_months | Query:: bottle_feeding_faq] Answer: The use of a bottle nipple can interfere with a newborn's ability to suckle effectively at the breast. It is also more difficult to clean a bottle and nipple thoroughly, which can pass germs to the infant. It is recommended to use a clean cup or spoon instead.
</qa_pair>
</secondary_faqs_block>
