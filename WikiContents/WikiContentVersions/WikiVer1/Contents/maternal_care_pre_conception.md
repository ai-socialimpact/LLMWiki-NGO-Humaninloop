## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer
<executive_brief_block>
### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: pre_conception | Milestone:: planning | Scope:: overview] Pre-conception care focuses on optimizing a woman's health before pregnancy to ensure the best possible outcome for both mother and child. Key recommendations include achieving an ideal Body Mass Index (BMI) between 18.5 and 23 kg/m² and ensuring the woman is at least 21 years of age. Essential pre-pregnancy health checks involve screening for hemoglobin levels to detect anemia, blood pressure, thyroid status, and blood glucose. A critical nutritional intervention is the daily supplementation of folic acid, with a recommended intake of 400 micrograms starting at least three months before conception to prevent neural tube defects in the fetus. A balanced diet rich in iron, vitamin B12, and other micronutrients is crucial for building nutritional reserves. Lifestyle modifications are mandatory, including the complete avoidance of alcohol, tobacco, and unprescribed medications. Adherence to these guidelines helps establish a healthy foundation for pregnancy and supports optimal fetal development from the very beginning.

### C. Contrastive Boundary Analysis (What This Document Is NOT)
[Target:: pre_conception | Milestone:: trimester2 | Context:: temporal_contrast] TEMPORAL CONTRAST: Contrast the active timeline of this file against succeeding stages. While pre-conception care mandates folic acid supplementation (400 mcg), do NOT confuse this with the combination Iron and Folic Acid (IFA) tablets (60 mg elemental iron + 0.5mg folic acid) which are recommended from the 12th week of pregnancy onwards.
[Target:: pre_conception | Severity:: severe_anemia | Context:: intervention_contrast] SEVERITY CONTRAST: Contrast routine pre-conception planning against emergency triggers. While routine planning involves dietary improvements for mild anemia, a pre-conception diagnosis of severe anemia with Hemoglobin below 7 g/dL is a critical condition requiring immediate medical referral and intensive treatment, not just dietary advice.
