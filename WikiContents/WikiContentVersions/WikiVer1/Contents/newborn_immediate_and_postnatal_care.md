## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer
<executive_brief_block>
### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: newborn | Milestone:: birth_to_28_days | Scope:: overview] This document outlines the essential care protocols for a newborn from the moment of birth up to 28 days of age. Immediate actions at birth include wiping the baby dry and clean, delaying the first bath for at least 24 hours, and initiating skin-to-skin contact to ensure thermal regulation. Umbilical cord care requires keeping the cord dry without applying any substances. The initial immunization schedule must be strictly followed, with BCG, OPV-0 (zero dose), and the Hepatitis B birth dose administered at specific times. Early initiation of breastfeeding, within the first hour of birth, is critical to provide the newborn with colostrum, which acts as the first immunization. For stable low birth weight (LBW) infants, Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) is a high-impact intervention involving prolonged skin-to-skin contact and exclusive breastfeeding to improve survival and health outcomes. Caregivers must be educated on newborn danger signs, such as poor feeding or high temperature, and know when to seek immediate medical care.

Similarly, routine thermal care is for stable infants; a baby with hypothermia requires emergency medical intervention.

### D. Important Facts Summary
[Target:: newborn | Milestone:: at_birth | Metric:: vaccination] The Hepatitis B birth dose (0.5 ml) should be given intramuscularly within 24 hours of birth. (Source: IMMUNISATION QUESTIONS FOR CHATBOT.md)
[Target:: newborn | Milestone:: birth_to_28_days | Metric:: vaccination] The OPV-0 (zero dose) vaccine, consisting of 2 oral drops, should be given at birth or within the first 15 days. (Source: IMMUNISATION QUESTIONS FOR CHATBOT.md)
[Target:: newborn | Milestone:: birth_to_28_days | Metric:: vaccination] The BCG vaccine should be given at birth or as early as possible, up to one year of age. The dose is 0.1 ml (or 0.05 ml for infants under one month) administered intradermally. (Source: IMMUNISATION QUESTIONS FOR CHATBOT.md)
[Target:: newborn | Milestone:: at_birth | Action:: thermal_care] Immediately after birth, the baby should be wiped dry and clean, and the first bath should be delayed for at least 24 hours to maintain warmth. (Source: CHILD CARE AFTER BIRTH.md)
[Target:: newborn | Milestone:: birth_to_28_days | Action:: cord_care] The umbilical cord should be kept dry, and no substances, such as ointment, should be applied to it. (Source: CHILD CARE AFTER BIRTH.md)

[Target:: newborn | Milestone:: at_birth | Query:: vaccination_timing_faq] Answer: The Hepatitis B birth dose should be given within 24 hours of birth. For maximum protection against mother-to-child transmission, it is best if given within 12 hours of birth.

[Target:: newborn | Milestone:: birth_to_28_days | Query:: vaccination_reaction_faq] Answer: Yes, scarring is a normal reaction after BCG administration. About two weeks after the injection, the site usually becomes red and hard, then forms a small elevation that may crack and discharge pus before healing into a small scar after about 8 weeks.
