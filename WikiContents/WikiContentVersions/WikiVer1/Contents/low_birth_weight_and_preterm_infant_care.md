## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer
<executive_brief_block>
### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: lbw_care | Milestone:: overview | Scope:: summary] This module provides specialized care protocols for low birth weight (LBW, <2.5kg) and preterm infants. A central intervention is Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC), a high-impact method for stable infants weighing less than 2000g, which can prevent up to half of all deaths in this group. KMC involves prolonged skin-to-skin contact and exclusive, frequent breastfeeding, which helps stabilize body temperature, prevent infection, promote weight gain, and foster bonding. Feeding LBW infants presents unique challenges due to their physical immaturity, including an inability to coordinate sucking, swallowing, and breathing. While direct breastfeeding is the ultimate goal, alternative methods using expressed breast milk are often necessary. These methods include feeding with a cup, spoon, or paladai, and in some cases, an oro-gastric tube. The use of feeding bottles is discouraged. Growth monitoring for LBW infants follows specific standards distinct from those for full-term babies. An initial weight loss of up to 10-15% is considered normal, with birth weight typically regained by two weeks. Subsequently, a daily weight gain of 15-20 grams per kilogram of body weight is the target. Adequacy of nutrition is monitored by tracking daily weight gain and ensuring a urine output of 8-12 times per day.

### C. Contrastive Boundary Analysis (What This Document Is NOT)
[Target:: lbw_care | Milestone:: kmc | Context:: intervention_contrast] KMC VS. ROUTINE SKIN-TO-SKIN: Do not confuse Kangaroo Mother Care (KMC) with routine skin-to-skin care at birth. While routine skin-to-skin is recommended for all newborns immediately after delivery, KMC is a specific, sustained, long-duration intervention for stable Low Birth Weight (LBW) infants.
[Target:: lbw_care | Milestone:: growth_monitoring | Context:: population_contrast] LBW VS. STANDARD GROWTH MONITORING: Do not apply standard WHO growth charts for healthy infants to this population. LBW infants have specific growth targets, including an expected initial weight loss of 10-15% and a subsequent daily gain of 15-20 g/kg of body weight. Infants under 1500g require a postnatal growth chart until they reach 2500g.
[Target:: lbw_care | Milestone:: feeding | Context:: method_contrast] DIRECT VS. ALTERNATIVE FEEDING: While direct breastfeeding is the goal, do not assume all LBW infants can achieve it immediately. Due to weakness or illness, many require alternative methods like a cup, spoon, or paladai to be fed expressed breast milk. Feeding bottles are actively discouraged.
