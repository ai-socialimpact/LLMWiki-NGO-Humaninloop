### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: local_health_services | Milestone:: mumbai | Scope:: overview] The Municipal Corporation of Greater Mumbai (MCGM) operates a comprehensive public health infrastructure across an area of 434 sq km. This network includes 4 medical college hospitals, 1 dental college hospital, 16 municipal general hospitals, 6 specialty hospitals, 29 maternity homes, 175 municipal dispensaries, and 183 health posts. These facilities provide a range of services, including primary, secondary, and tertiary care. Specific schedules are in place for key services at various health posts. For instance, in Bhiwandi, health posts like Auchitpada HP and Gaibi Nagar HP offer ANC check-ups and Hb tests on specific weekdays, with immunization sessions held every Tuesday. In the P/N ward, facilities like Choksi Maternity Home in Malad (West) provide maternal and neonatal care. The Healthy Cities Program includes Health Post 1, Health Post 2, and Rathodi Health Post, which conduct Hb tests every Thursday and immunization every Saturday, with ANC clinics scheduled on Mondays or Thursdays depending on the location. These institutions also facilitate access to national and state-level government schemes such as the Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK), Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY), and Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY), providing free services, diagnostics, and financial benefits to eligible pregnant women, mothers, and infants.
