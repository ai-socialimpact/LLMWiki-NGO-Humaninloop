## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer

### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: trimester2 | Milestone:: weeks_13_28 | Scope:: overview] The second trimester of pregnancy, spanning weeks 13 to 28, is a critical period for monitoring fetal growth and initiating key prophylactic health interventions. The second mandatory antenatal care (ANC) check-up is scheduled between weeks 16 and 24. A crucial protocol initiated during this trimester is the daily supplementation with Iron and Folic Acid (IFA) tablets, starting from week 14-16, to prevent maternal anemia and support fetal development. Concurrently, twice-daily calcium carbonate supplementation begins to support fetal bone development and prevent maternal hypertension. A single-dose deworming protocol using a 400 mg Albendazole tablet is administered between weeks 14 and 16. A key diagnostic milestone is the fetal anomaly ultrasound scan, performed between weeks 18 and 22 to assess for structural abnormalities. Nutritional requirements increase, with a recommended additional intake of 350 calories and 8 grams of protein per day starting from the second trimester.
