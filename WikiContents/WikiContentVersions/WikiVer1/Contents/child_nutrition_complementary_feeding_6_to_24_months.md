## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer

### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: complementary_feeding | Milestone:: 6_24_months | Action:: overview] Complementary feeding is the process of introducing other foods and liquids alongside breast milk, starting from the age of 6 months, as breast milk alone is no longer sufficient to meet the nutritional needs of a growing child. This period, from 6 to 24 months, is part of the critical "1000 days" window for a child's growth and development. The introduction should be gradual, starting with pureed, mashed, and semi-solid foods at 6 months, progressing to "finger foods" around 8 months, and incorporating family foods by the end of the first year. The feeding schedule evolves with age: from 2-3 meals of 2-3 tablespoons each for infants 6-8 months old; to 3 meals plus 1-2 snacks of about half a cup (125 mL) for infants 9-11 months old; and finally to 3-4 meals plus 1-2 snacks of ¾ to one full cup (250 mL) for children 12-24 months old. Key principles include ensuring dietary diversity by offering foods from at least four different food groups, practicing responsive feeding by feeding patiently without force and avoiding distractions like screens, and maintaining strict hygiene. Home-prepared, locally available foods like khichdi, dal-rice, and ragi are recommended, while processed, sugary, and fried foods should be avoided. Myths regarding 'hot' and 'cold' foods should be disregarded, and a variety of nutrient-dense foods should be offered to support optimal health.

[Target:: complementary_feeding | Milestone:: 6_24_months | Query:: nutrition_enhancement_faq] Question: How can I make my baby's food more nutritious and energy-dense?
[Target:: complementary_feeding | Milestone:: 6_24_months | Query:: nutrition_enhancement_faq] Answer: You can increase the energy density by adding a small amount of ghee, sugar, jaggery, vegetable oils, or butter. Foods like eggs, chicken, curd, and bananas are highly nutritious and should be included in your child's diet for proper growth and development.
</qa_pair>
