## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer
<executive_brief_block>
### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: maternal_care_t1 | Milestone:: overview | Action:: summary] The first trimester of pregnancy, from confirmation through week 12, focuses on early registration and the initiation of antenatal care (ANC). Pregnant women are advised to register their pregnancy at the nearest government hospital or healthcare center within the first 12 weeks. The first ANC check-up, also conducted within this period, includes essential baseline assessments such as weight monitoring, blood pressure checks, and a series of diagnostic tests. These initial tests screen for blood group and Rh compatibility, hemoglobin levels (anemia), urine protein and sugar, and various infections like HIV, Syphilis, and Hepatitis B & C. An early ultrasound between 6-9 weeks is recommended to confirm gestational age and fetal viability. A critical intervention is the daily supplementation of 500 mcg of Folic Acid to prevent neural tube defects. The first dose of the Tetanus-Diphtheria (TD) vaccine is also administered as soon as pregnancy is confirmed. Dietary guidance emphasizes a balanced diet, increased fluid intake, and managing nausea by consuming small, frequent meals. Government schemes like Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana (PMMVY) provide a financial benefit of Rs 3,000 for early registration and ANC, while Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK) ensures that all services, including tests and transport, are free at government facilities.

### C. Contrastive Boundary Analysis (What This Document Is NOT)
[Target:: maternal_care_t1 | Milestone:: overview | Context:: temporal_contrast] TEMPORAL CONTRAST: This document covers care up to week 12. It mandates Folic Acid supplementation only. Do NOT confuse this with the combined Iron-Folic Acid (IFA) tablets, which are recommended to start from the 12th or 14th week of pregnancy (Trimester 2). Similarly, deworming with Albendazole is a Trimester 2 intervention (4th month) and is not covered here.
[Target:: maternal_care_t1 | Milestone:: overview | Context:: intervention_contrast] INTERVENTION CONTRAST: This document details routine initial screenings and care. While it includes a baseline hemoglobin (Hb) test, it does NOT cover therapeutic regimens for anemia (e.g., 2 IFA tablets daily, iron sucrose injections). Cases of severe anemia (Hb < 7 g/dL) or findings like an ectopic pregnancy on an early ultrasound are emergencies requiring immediate referral to a higher facility and are beyond the scope of these routine protocols.

### E. Document Context & Validity Rules (When This Information Holds Good)
[Target:: maternal_care_t1 | Milestone:: overview | Action:: validity] This document is valid for all pregnant women from the point of pregnancy confirmation up to the completion of the 12th week of gestation. The guidelines herein apply to the first antenatal care visit, initial health screenings, nutritional advice specific to early pregnancy, and the initial vaccination schedule. This information is intended to guide community health workers (ASHA, ANM, AWW) and pregnant women on the standard procedures for a healthy start to pregnancy. The document becomes invalid for any care decisions, supplementation, or testing scheduled for the second or third trimesters.
