## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer
<executive_brief_block>
### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: jssk | Milestone:: scheme_overview | Action:: summary] The Janani Shishu Suraksha Karyakram (JSSK) is a government scheme designed to provide completely free services to pregnant women and sick infants at government health institutions. For pregnant women, the scheme covers all expenses related to delivery, including free normal and caesarean deliveries, free medicines and consumables, free diagnostic tests like blood tests and sonography, and free blood transfusions if required. It also guarantees free nutritional meals during their stay and free transport from home to the facility, between facilities for referrals, and back home. The duration of free stay and meals is 3 days for a normal delivery and 7 days for a caesarean delivery. For sick children under 1 year of age, JSSK provides free treatment, medicines, diagnostics, nutritional meals, and transport, ensuring exemption from all hospital-related charges. The program's objective is to eliminate out-of-pocket expenditure for maternal and infant care.
