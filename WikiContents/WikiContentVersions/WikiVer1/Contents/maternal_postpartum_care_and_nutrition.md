## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer
<executive_brief_block>
### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: postpartum_care | Milestone:: 0_6_months | Action:: overview] This document outlines the essential health and nutritional guidelines for a mother during the first six months postpartum. A primary focus is on the increased nutritional demands for lactation and recovery, requiring an additional 600 calories and 13.6g of protein daily. It is critical to continue supplementation with Iron and Folic Acid (IFA) for at least 100 days and up to six months postpartum to replenish iron stores and prevent anemia, with a daily calcium intake of 1000 mg to support bone health and milk production. Physical recovery is supported by immediate breastfeeding, which aids uterine contraction and reduces blood loss, along with adequate rest and avoidance of strenuous activity. The guidelines also cover managing breastfeeding with common maternal illnesses like fever or while on medication for conditions such as hypothyroidism. Emotional well-being is supported through the bonding experience of breastfeeding, which can help mothers overcome postpartum depression. Adherence to food safety, adequate fluid intake of over 2 liters per day, and a diverse, nutrient-dense diet are foundational for a healthy postpartum period for the mother.

[Target:: postpartum_care | Milestone:: 0_6_months | Query:: supplement_faq] Answer: There are two recommended protocols.
