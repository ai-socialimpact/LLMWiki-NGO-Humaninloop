## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer
<executive_brief_block>
### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: home_safety | Milestone:: overview | Action:: summary] This document provides a comprehensive set of guidelines for establishing and maintaining a safe and hygienic home environment, with a strong focus on prevention. Key protocols include personal hygiene practices, such as mandatory handwashing with soap and water before handling babies, preparing food, eating, and after using the toilet. For food safety, it specifies rules for safe storage, preparation, and handling, including temperature and time limits for cooked food (not at room temperature for over 2 hours, not in the fridge for more than 2 days), thorough cooking, and proper reheating. Safe water handling is addressed, including the use of clean water, prevention of contamination, and managing fluoride levels. The guide details extensive measures for preventing common household accidents, particularly for children, such as securing open water sources, storing toxic substances out of reach, and keeping children away from hazards like sharp objects and hot surfaces. Finally, it mandates the creation of a smoke-free environment, prohibiting smoke from tobacco, cooking, and heating to protect all household members, especially pregnant women and young children.

[Target:: home_safety | Milestone:: accident_prevention | Query:: choking_faq] Answer: Avoid giving young children foods that can get lodged in their windpipe, such as whole nuts, grapes, and raw carrots. Nuts should be finely ground before being mixed into food.

[Target:: home_safety | Milestone:: water_safety | Query:: fluoride_faq] Answer: The safe level of fluoride in drinking water should be less than 1.5 mg/L. If your water has higher levels, you should use a water filter specifically designed to remove fluoride.
