## 2. Executive Guidance & Context Layer

### A. Long Comprehensive Summary
[Target:: child_danger_signs | Milestone:: emergency_symptoms | Action:: overview] This document outlines the critical danger signs in newborns and children under five years of age that necessitate immediate medical care. Based on health guidance, caregivers must be able to recognize these symptoms and know where to seek help. The primary danger signs identified include the child not feeding well, not eating or drinking enough, exhibiting fast breathing, having a high temperature, or being less active than usual. The presence of any of these signs indicates a potential serious illness, and the explicit instruction is to see or contact a health worker as soon as possible. This protocol is strictly for emergency symptom recognition and does not cover routine child care, which must be sourced from other guidelines.
